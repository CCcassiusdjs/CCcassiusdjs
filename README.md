## 🔬 Research Projects

### AIM-FTA — CNPq 407477/2022-5
*Assessment, Identification, and Mitigation of Soft Errors in Avionics Embedded Software*

Fault injection and tolerance analysis in safety-critical avionics software. The project aims to develop methodologies and tools for assessing the reliability of embedded software in aerospace systems through virtual fault injection platforms. I work on extending fault injection capabilities for ArduPilot SITL (Software-in-the-Loop) simulation to evaluate fault tolerance mechanisms in unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).

**Key activities:**
- Fork of ArduPilot for fault injection experiments
- SITL-based fault injection campaigns
- Single-event upset (SEU) emulation in flight control algorithms
- Collaboration with Loughborough University, UFRGS, and INSTOR

> **PI:** Prof. Ney Laert Vilar Calazans · **Role:** Scientific Initiation Fellow since May 2025

---

### Projeto Jundiá — CNPq/MCTI/FNDCT N° 25/2025
*Advanced Simulation Platform for Autonomous Vessels in Offshore Environments with Digital Twin Support*

Development of a high-fidelity simulation infrastructure for autonomous maritime vessels operating in offshore environments. The project integrates physical modeling, digital twins, and fault simulation to enable realistic testing of autonomous navigation algorithms under adverse conditions. I contribute to Team B (Fault Tolerance & Integration), focusing on sensor degradation modeling, contingency resource management, and fault injection in maritime simulations.

**Key activities:**
- Robot simulator fork for maritime vessel dynamics
- 3D Digital Twin visualization for drone swarms
- Integration of ROS 2 navigation stacks with Webots simulator
- Fault injection for sensor degradation scenarios
- Collaboration with Instor, PUCRS, UFSC, and Loughborough University

> **PI:** Prof. Anderson Domingues · **Role:** ITI-A Scientific Initiation Fellow (Mar 2025 – Mar 2026)

---

### SleepSensing — FAPERGS/FAPESP 2025–Present
*Photoplethysmography Sensing and Artificial Intelligence Application for the SleepingDreamSystem*

Complete pipeline for sleep apnea detection using photoplethysmography (PPG) signals and deep learning. The project develops an end-to-end system for non-invasive sleep apnea screening using wearable PPG sensors, eliminating the need for expensive and uncomfortable polysomnography (PSG) exams. I built the complete data processing and model training pipeline at LSE (Laboratório de Sistemas Embarcados).

**Key activities:**
- ISRUC dataset preparation: standardization, signal parsing, annotation alignment
- Signal preprocessing: filtering, normalization, artifact removal
- Temporal windowing with configurable window sizes and overlap
- CRNN (Convolutional Recurrent Neural Network) with attention mechanism
- Model training with checkpoint support, early stopping, and hyperparameter tuning
- Signal caching system for efficient data loading

**Technical stack:**
- 🧠 **Model:** CRNN with attention (PyTorch)
- 📊 **Dataset:** ISRUC-Sleep (multi-channel PSG adapted for PPG)
- 🔧 **Signal processing:** FFT, wavelets, bandpass filtering, HRV analysis
- 🎯 **Output:** Per-epoch apnea detection (30-second windows)

> **PI:** Prof. César Augusto Missio Marcon · **Role:** Scientific Initiation Fellow @ LSE

### Mixed Reality for Inclusive Education — CNPq 2022–2023
*Science and Mathematics Learning of Inclusion Students in Basic Education: Implications from the Design and Development of Mixed Reality Applications*

Research and development of Mixed Reality (MR) applications for inclusive mathematics and science education targeting students with disabilities. The project investigated how MR technologies (augmented and virtual reality) can support students with developmental dyscalculia, cerebral palsy, and visual impairments in understanding abstract mathematical and scientific concepts.

**Key activities:**
- Systematic literature review on MR applications for dyscalculia
- Design of AR-based geometry learning activities
- User studies with students with special needs
- Accessibility evaluation of MR interfaces
- Focus on elementary education (ages 7-14)

**Target disabilities:**
- 🧮 Developmental Dyscalculia (mathematical learning difficulties)
- ♿ Cerebral Palsy (motor impairments)
- 👁️ Visual Impairment (low vision, blindness)

**Publication:**
> *Mixed Reality Applications for Students with Developmental Dyscalculia in Elementary Education: A Literature Review* — Nuevas Ideas en Informática Educativa, December 2023.

> **PI:** Prof. Isabel Cristina Machado de Lara · **Role:** Scientific Initiation Fellow
